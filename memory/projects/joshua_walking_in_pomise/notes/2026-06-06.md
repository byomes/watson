Sermon 01

Series Title: 
Joshua: Walking In Promise

Key Thought: 
God's promises never expire. They are received by walking in faithful obedience.

Message Title: 
Victory Through Obedience
Joshua 1:1-18

A: Godly leadership operates within covenant.

1 After the death of Moses the servant of the LORD, the LORD said to Joshua son of Nun, Moses’ aide: 

	After the death - leadership transition 
	
	Servant… LORD - covenant framework 
	
	LORD said to Joshua - new covenant leader 


2 “Moses my servant is dead. Now then, you and all these people, get ready to cross the Jordan River into the land I am about to give to them—to the Israelites. 

	My servant is dead - old seasons die, this is a part of the journey, and because of Moses's disobedience
	
	Now then - what we do at transition matters 
	
	You and all these people - the promise remained, it was more than Moses, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, or Joseph

	Cross the Jordan - the promised land had clear boundaries
	
	I am about to give - the promise was always from God to the people


3 I will give you every place where you set your foot, as I promised Moses. 

	I will give you - the promise land was received from God, not achieved by force
	
	As I promised Moses - God's faithfulness to His Word leads the way, God's Word is guaranteed by His character. 


God's faithfulness flows from His nature, not our performance. 


4 Your territory will extend from the desert to Lebanon, and from the great river, the Euphrates—all the Hittite country—to the Mediterranean Sea in the west. 

	Your territory - this goes back to God's promise to Abraham in Genesis 15; the land is because of the Covenant! 

The great Christian hope of eternity with Christ is because of Covenant, not our performance or preference! 


5 No one will be able to stand against you all the days of your life. As I was with Moses, so I will be with you; I will never leave you nor forsake you. 

	No one will be able to - this is because of God, not Joshua
	
	Stand against you - God's presence and leadership did not do away with opposition

	As I was with Moses - God gives Joshua a frame for His relationship from the model of Moses 
	
	Will never leave you - this is God's faithfulness, not Joshua's worthiness 

Application:
Where are you leading others right now (family, work, church, school, etc.) and are you leading from God's Word or from your own instincts?

What would it look like for your leadership to be shaped more by God's covenant than by your own expectations?



B: Obedience is how covenant victory is received.

6 Be strong and courageous, because you will lead these people to inherit the land I swore to their ancestors to give them. 

	Be strong and courageous - think Deuteronomy 31
	
	You will lead the people - Joshua's leadership is his responsibility, the inheritance is not

Remember: Joshua just lived through the wilderness wandering! The last 40 years of his life have experienced the failure of disobedience. 


7 “Be strong and very courageous. Be careful to obey all the law my servant Moses gave you; do not turn from it to the right or to the left, that you may be successful wherever you go. 

	Be strong and courageous - previous verse 
	
	Be careful - lit. "keep watch; guard; watch over" there is a protective sense to the 'watching' 
	
	To obey - lit. "to make, manufacture, cause, to make physical" 
	
	All the law - the whole of the God's Law

	Right / left - there is more than one way to disobey…

	That you may be successful - God directly tied the success of their inheritance FROM Him to their OBEDIENCE to Him


8 Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips; meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do everything written in it. Then you will be prosperous and successful. 

	Keep - the verb tense here is ongoing / habitual 
	
	Book of the Law - the complete Words from God at the time
	
	On your lips / meditate on it - the Word of God should be visible in the inner and outer workings of our life
	
	So that you  - centering one's life and self on the Word is the mechanism which builds Godly obedience 

		Romans 12:2
Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind. Then you will be able to test and approve what God’s will is—his good, pleasing and perfect will.


9 Have I not commanded you? Be strong and courageous. Do not be afraid; do not be discouraged, for the LORD your God will be with you wherever you go.” 

	Have I not commanded you? - literary device "say it again for the people in the back"
	
	Be strong and courageous - in your obedience 

	Do not be afraid - fear is still a real thing
	
	Do not be discouraged - discouragement is still a real thing
	
	For the LORD - the reason is God's covenant promise and presence

Does your relationship with God drown out fear and discouragement? Or do they drown out the voice of God in your life? 

Application:
Is there an area of your life where you are waiting on God to move, but God may be waiting on your obedience?

What verse, promise, or prayer are you holding onto right now, and what does obedience look like in it?


C: God's promises are not just personal, they are corporate. 

10 So Joshua ordered the officers of the people: 

	Joshua ordered the officers - this is Joshua actively doing what God has called him to do


11 “Go through the camp and tell the people, ‘Get your provisions ready. Three days from now you will cross the Jordan here to go in and take possession of the land the LORD your God is giving you for your own.’ ” 

	Get your provisions ready - just because God was going before them didn't mean they didn't need to plan ahead
	
	Go in and take possession - they were receiving, not taking
	
	God is giving you - God is the land owner, Israel is the inheritor 


12 But to the Reubenites, the Gadites and the half-tribe of Manasseh, Joshua said, 

13 “Remember the command that Moses the servant of the LORD gave you after he said, ‘The LORD your God will give you rest by giving you this land.’ 

14 Your wives, your children and your livestock may stay in the land that Moses gave you east of the Jordan, but all your fighting men, ready for battle, must cross over ahead of your fellow Israelites. You are to help them 

15 until the LORD gives them rest, as he has done for you, and until they too have taken possession of the land the LORD your God is giving them. After that, you may go back and occupy your own land, which Moses the servant of the LORD gave you east of the Jordan toward the sunrise.” 

	East of the Jordan - these tribes were IN their promised land, but couldn't rest while their brothers hadn't received their inheritance yet

Application:
Is there someone in your life (family, friend, church, school, work, etc.) whose inheritance you could be helping secure right now?

Are you so settled in your own blessing that you have stopped fighting for the people around you?


D: Covenant obedience is all-in obedience. 

16 Then they answered Joshua, “Whatever you have commanded us we will do, and wherever you send us we will go. 

	Whatever / wherever - the people respond fully with a commitment to obey the Lord and Joshua leading them 


17 Just as we fully obeyed Moses, so we will obey you. Only may the LORD your God be with you as he was with Moses. 

	Only may the LORD - this is a restriction that they would follow Joshua as long as Joshua followed God, as Moses did


18 Whoever rebels against your word and does not obey it, whatever you may command them, will be put to death. Only be strong and courageous!”

	Whoever rebels - they recognized collective obedience would be tested 
	
	Put to death - this is the same generation that just survived the wilderness wandering… the KNOW obedience is the way to victory and blessing; disobedience will bring delay and death. 

Application:
Where in your life are you offering God partial obedience and calling it enough?

What would all-in obedience look like for you in this season, and what is keeping you from it?


The Takeaway: 
God's promises are ours to walk into, not simply wait on.
