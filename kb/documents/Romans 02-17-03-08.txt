Observation W verse Additional notes
Now you who Rom 2:17 Paul's next argument begins by targetting a specific group of people, he will use several descriptors to identify them
- call yourself a Jew who Rom 2:17 the people to whom this point will be made are those who consider themselves to be Jews
- rely on the Law who Rom 2:17 Jews of Jesus's and Paul's day relied on the presence (and supposed obedience) to the Law for their claim to righteousness
- boast in God who Rom 2:17 the Jews would often boast that they are the chosen people of God. This is both true and misleading. The Jews are God's chosen
people THROUGH WHOM salvation came to the world, they are not saved because they chosen.
- know His will / approve what Rom 2:18 the Jews not only "knew" the will of God, but to approve (scrutinize and recognize what is genuine) God will from man's will
- instrcuted in the Law what Rom 2:18 The Jews had already received special revelation from God in the OT Scriptures, they knew God and His will for them
- convinced you are a guide what Rom 2:19 the Jews to which Paul is addressing not only knew the Law, but accepted the responsibility to lead others in obeying it
- a light for those in the dark what Rom 2:19 light is often a symbol of God's presenece and/or revelation (John 1:1-13) these Jewish believers had the light of the OT
Scriptures and the indwelling of the Holy Spirit (remember, he is writing to Jewish & Gentile Christians)
- an instructor of the foolish what Rom 2:20 adding to the knowledge of God and the responsibility to teach, is the idea the teacher "knows more" than the student
- teacher of little children what Rom 2:20 doubling down on the attitude of the "teacher / student" relationship, now the teacher is greater than "little children" the word
carries not just the connotation of age, but also of immaturity, the people Paul is addressing see themselves as more mature
- because you have the Law what Rom 2:20 the reason for the Jews supposed "higher maturity" is their posession of the Law, not their own righteousness
you, then what Rom 2:21 this point the argument begins to be made, the previous verses describe the type of person, the next few describe their actions
- teach / teach yourselves what Rom 2:21 Paul now uses the glowing description of the Jewish believers in the previous verses to point our their hypocrisy. They have
the OT Law, teach it to the Gentiles as it should be taught, but do not obey it themselves. The definition of hypocrisy.
- preach against stealing / stea what Rom 2:22 the accusation of stealing is a clear violation of the law and one of the "big 10" commandments
- not commit adultry / commit what Rom 2:22 the accusation of adultery is the same as the previous, a violation of the law and another of the "big 10"
- abhor idols / rob temples what Rom 2:22 idolatry is another of the "big 10" sins, yet they are willing to "rob temples" or commit sacriledge against God
- boast in the Law / break it what Rom 2:23 for all their righteous brovado, the hypocritical Jews believes here are not living the faith they say they practice
God's name is blasphemed what Rom 2:24 quoting from either Isaiah 52:5 or Ezekiel 36:20,22 Paul reminds the Jewish Christians who had the OT Scriptures that their
lifestyle and actions are not honoring to God, but actually the cause of His name being dishonored.
Circumcision has value what Rom 2:25 remember, this was the sign of the covenant given to Abraham (Gen 17) this sign was at the heart of the Jews view of themselves
- if you obey what Rom 2:25 what the Jews of this day did not understand is the Abrahamic Covenant was unconditional in its scope (God's character
guarantees it will be fulfilled) but it is conditional in its application to the individual, they had to obey to be blessed.
If you break the Law what Rom 2:25 there is a big difference between knowing the Law and obeying the Law. The heart must choose to obey what the mind knows
- become… not circumcised what Rom 2:25 the issue of obedience (Rom 1:5) is the stumbling block for these Jewish believers. Trusting in their heritage is not enough
those who are not circumcised who Rom 2:26 the gentile Christians at Rome who have had leadership of the church for 5 or so years due to Emporer Claudius
- keep the Law's requirements what Rom 2:26 the reversal of the situation here, those who are Gentiles actually "live out" the Law better than those who are Jews
- regarded as circumcised what Rom 2:26 and here we see the issue of "obedience through faith" preceed and overshadow the Law itself. The point Paul is making is
one well founded in Scripture: "Faith makes one righteous" Romans 4:9, 22; Galatians 3:6; James 2:23; Genesis 15:6
- will condemn you what Rom 2:27 Paul makes the bold point that those who live in obedience will condemn those who live in legalism
Not a Jew outwardly who Rom 2:28 outward actions like legalistic obedience and physical circumscision do not make one righteous or a part of God's people
- physical circumcision what Rom 2:28 the eternal sign of circumcision is the symbol of the "outwardly" focused faith
Is a Jew inwardly who Rom 2:29 the inward attitude of the heart of obedience is what makes on righteous and a part of God's people
- circumcision of the heart what Rom 2:29 the "sign" of a righteous and redeemed life is the heart that is obediently faithful to the Lord
praise from people / God what Rom 2:29 Paul makes the illustration that God praises the inward heart while people praise the outward acts
what advantage what Rom 3:1 not wanting to leave the argument seeming to conclude that there is no point to being a Jew anymore, Paul is addressing
hypocrisy and faith, now defends the value of being faithful AND a member of God's chosen people
Jews who Rom 3:1 the chosen people of God, descended from Abraham, reciptients of the covenants from God Himself
- entrusted with the Word what Rom 3:2 one way in which the Jews are to be respected is they were the chosen people to whom God revealed His nature and Word
some where unfaithful who Rom 3:3 Paul admits that there are Jews, members of God's chosen people, who have been unfaithful to Him
nullify God's faithfulness what Rom 3:3 just because some of God's chosen people strayed from faithfulness does not mean God Himself has. The implication is that
God will hold true to His promises to both the Jews and the Gentile Christians
unrighteousness / righteousne what Rom 3:5 Paul is alluding to his previous point, man's unfaithfulness to God's Law just magnifies the greatness of His faithfulness
God is unjust in bringing wrath what Rom 3:5 is God unjust by bringing wrath on our sin, even when our sin magnifies His grace? No, sin still brings death and judgment
why am I still condemned what Rom 3:7 Paul is making an argument likely made or at least inferred by the Jewish hypocrites he has been addressing. Their position
is that if their sin magnifies God's grace, than doesn't that make their sin a good thing? No, not it does not.
Let us do evil… good may resul what Rom 3:8 this is a lie that so evil, yet people still fall pray to it. Chasing sin and evil does not glorify God, it condemns us.
