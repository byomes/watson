 |  |  | own, but only if He is circumcised. This does not relate to his salvation in any way. This is why Paul will later defend Titus'
 |  |  | right to not need to be circucised, because Titus was a Greek (Galatians 2:3-5) This may also allude to Paul's understanding 
 |  |  | that the OT promises set forth by the Lord are still active and circumcision was the sign of the Abrahamic Covenant 
 |  |  | Because Timothy's father was a Greek, this would also have given Timothy a great advantage in ministering to Greeks also

 |  |  | we continue to read the section we see that by verse 9, Paul has a vision calling him to Macedonia. In my (pastor Bill) opinion
 |  |  | the best reason for why the Holy Spirit restrains Paul and Silas' ministry efforts is because they have a divine appointment 
 |  |  | to carry the Gospel to europe
Spirit of Jesus | who | Acts 16:7 | this verse illustrates and early church belief in the divinity of Jesus as Lord and God
