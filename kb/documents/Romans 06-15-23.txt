Observation W verse Additional notes
what then what Rom 16:15 Paul begins this next section of his presentation standing on the point of the previous verses. As Christians we are called
to count ourselves as "dead to sin" and "alive in Christ". This requires us to offer ourselves to God rather than sin.
shall we sin? what Rom 16:15 Protecting against a dangerous heresy, Paul addresses a possible twisting of his teaching. Yes, we are dead to sin. Yes, we are
alive in Christ. NO, this DOES NOT mean we are permitted to sin as much as we want!
- not under law, under grace where Rom 16:15 here again we see the "changing of the head" for Christians from being a people who are "under the law" or the old system
of guilt and shame to being "under grace" the new system of forgiveness and purity. Christians are still required to avoid sin
inpurity, wickedness, and the like. The difference is we do it because we are "owned" by grace, not by sin
offer yourselves… as… slaves what Rom 16:16 slavery was an institution in the first century, people were easily bought and sold, their autonomy as a person was never
questioned, though their freedom was. A person who is owned by another is autonomous, but is not free unto themselves.
- slaves to the one you obey who Rom 16:16 Paul uses the analogy of slavery to illustate "our actions reveal our owner" we are slaves of the "owner" we obey. There is
only two choices available in his analogy, either we are slaves of sin or God, there is no middle ground
- slaves to sin who Rom 16:16 the first of the two "slave" choices, we can be slaves to sin
- which leads to death where Rom 16:16 if we are slaves to sin, than we will "earn" the wage of death for sin, there is no way to get life from a wage of death
- slaves to obedience who Rom 16:16 something that is not talked about often in Christian circles is being a "slave to obedience". Paul is saying here, we are not
just a "slave to Christ" but that we are slaves to being OBEDIENT to Christ. Our lifestyle will illustrate who our real owner is
- which leads to righteousnewssho Rom 16:16 the second of the two "slave" choices leads us to righteousness or right standing with God, being "guilty, not guilty"
thanks be to God what Rom 16:17 the thanks and praise goes to God alone for the miracle of salvation and the ability to be forgiven, we did not earn it
used to be slaves to sin when Rom 16:17 notice the subtle time element here, Paul tells the Roman Christians they "used to be" slaves to sin. If you "used to be" one
thing, then the implication is you are no longer than thing now. There is a passage of time along with a change of state
- have come to obey what Rom 16:17 the illustration of truth faith is obedience to the teachings of Christ and the Scriptures. Salvation is received in faith, but it
is evidenced in our obedience to Christ and the Scriptures
- from your heart where Rom 16:17 our "slavery" to obedience begins in our heart. We must choose to submit our will and self to the reign of Christ
the pattern of teaching what Rom 16:17 Paul directly correlates the Apostles teaching as the object of which we are to be obedient. This doesn't allow for the option
of "choosing" who or what to obey. The early Christians were instructed by the Apostles in the teaching of the Lord. We today
have the canonnized Word of God, but we still are not permitted to pick and choose which of those we are going to obey
- claimed your allegience what Rom 16:17 our obedience and allegiance is to be held to the teaching of Christ and the Scriptures, not to anything or anyone else
set free… become slaves what Rom 16:18 again Paul hammers home the point, that we have become free of the bondage of sin, but in doing so have become slaves to
obedience to the teachings of Christ and the Scriptures. There is not a sense in which we are "free" to ourselves, we are "free"
only because a new master has purchased us from the old one. We are still slaves, just not to sin.
an example from life what Rom 16:19 Paul openly admits that this is an example by way of analogy, it is meant to be understood in the natural world first, then to
reveal a deeper spiritual truth.
just as you used to what Rom 16:19 this is quite possibly the most powerful five words in this passage. Paul instructs the first century Romans to commit
themselves to chasing after obedience and purity to the same measure as they once chased sin and impurity.
- offer yourselves what Rom 16:19 Paul again uses the imagery of an offering being made to an idol or false god. By chasing after sin and impurity, we are
unknowingly offering ourselves as idol offerings in worship of pleasure, greed, etc.
- as slaves to righteousness who Rom 16:19 we are now to offering ourselves as offerings to the One True God, and this offering is one of obedience
- leading to holiness where Rom 16:19 holiness, or the state of being set apart is the outcome of living a life of obedince. This is the "slavery" we are called to Q
when you were when Rom 16:20 Here, Paul doubles down on the "excluded middle" of the two slavery positions. We are either slaves to sin or righteousness
there is no middle ground to stand on in which we are "free" from both of them at the same time
- slaves to sin what Rom 16:20 described ealier, owned by and a lifestyle that evidences being owned by sin and wickedness
- free from… righteousness what Rom 16:20 being a slave to sin, is being free from righteousness, when one is "owned" by sin, righteousness has no rule in the life
- what benefit did you reapwhat Rom 16:21 Paul asks a powerful question, what benefit came from being a slave to sin. Temporary pleasure may have been a result
but that is fleeting and leaves on wanting, so it can hardly be considered consistant or ever-present, it's only fleeting
those things result in death what Rom 16:21 there is no room left for interpretation, slavery to sin, and the actions that inevitably follow, lead to death
but now when Rom 16:22 again, notice the subtle reference to time, "but now" illustrates the current state of affairs is different from what was before
set free from sin… slaves to Godwhat Rom 16:22 we are no longer slaves to sin, but to God, our obedience in life will either illusrate their truth or refute it
wages of sin… death what Rom 16:23 being a slave to sin will earn and only earn death, there is no "life" at the end of a life fulll on sin, only death
gift of life… Jesus Christ what Rom 16:23 being a slave to God will earn and only earn life, there is no "death" at the end of a life full of obedience, only life
