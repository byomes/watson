Observation W verse Additional notes
Therefore what Rom 12:1 because we cannot know the mind of God as His ways are so amazing as to work through the different "olive branches" to
bring about His mercy and grace in both the Jews and the Gentiles, because of this…
I urge you what Rom 12:1 Paul uses a word here that means to "call to one's side" or "comfort" He is calling the joint body of Roman Christians
- brothers and sisters who Rom 12:1 Paul describes the "you" he is addressing as people sharing the same ancestor, this is the joint body of Jews and Gentiles
both sharing the same ancestor in Abraham, the first covenant carrier and also first Gentile to put his faith in God
- the mercies of God what Rom 12:1 Paul uses a second descriptor of his "calling" of the Romans, this time in light of God's mercy/compassion
- offer your bodies what Rom 12:1 the "calling" is to offer a sacrifice to God, collectively as one tribe, in response or because of the compassion of God
- living sacrifice what Rom 12:1 the sacrifice that is being described is not a "one and done" kind as in the OT law, but a continual "living" sacrifice
- holy and pleasing what Rom 12:1 holy means to be "set apart" , the second descriptor is to be "pleasing or acceptable" in this case to God Almighty
- true and proper worship what Rom 12:1 the Greek word Paul uses here is /log-i-KOS/ (think the english word "logic") and is that which is true or reasonable. The call to
offer ourselves as living sacrifices to God is because this is the "logical" response to His mercy and compassion on us
do not conform what Rom 12:2 do not change one's shape or mold one's self to the another standard, part one of a two part command
- pattern of this world what Rom 12:2 the word "pattern" is not in the Greek, but it is a futher explination of the idea of conforming one's self. The earthly world
absolutely has a standard or pattern that it desires all men to follow, so does the Lord God, they are not the same thing
be transformed what Rom 12:2 part two of this command, be transformed. This implies that we are, at some level, already molded to this world. This can
and will be different for everyone. But, it is important to note that we must be self-aware of our "molding" to the world's ways
- by the renewing what Rom 12:2 the renewing here is that of a renovation or complete change for the better. No one expects a "renovated" house to be the same
- of your mind what Rom 12:2 the mind, or the immaterial center of one's being, the place of thought, feeling, understanding, judgment, etc
then when Rom 12:2 If we are able to complete the two-step process Paul describes, resisting the mold the world attempts to put us in AND renewing
our minds, THEN we will be able to do what Paul describes next
you will be able what Rom 12:2 this is a powerful promise, but it is one that is often misread or misunderstood. If we resist and renew we will be able to
- test and approve of what Rom 12:2 the word used her is what we today might refer to as a "scientific" word, it describes the testing or scrutinizing of something in
order to determine the genuine from the fake
- what God's will is what Rom 12:2 last chapter made a clear case for the will of God, this chapter we are given a promise that we are indeed able to know it
what Rom 12:2 the three descriptions here cannot be of God's will directly, as they would imply God's will could be anything other than
good, pleasing and perfect. Rather, they are an additional description of our actions, that God wishes for us to do, that which is
- good what Rom 12:2 the "good" is, in a Christian sense, that which is in line with God's character, as He is the ultimate "good" in creation
- pleasing what Rom 12:2 the "pleasing' is that which is pleasing TO GOD that WE DO. His will is not for our pleasure directly
- perfect what Rom 12:2 the "perfect" is not without fault, but that which is brought to completion. That which is whole, finished, and complete.
