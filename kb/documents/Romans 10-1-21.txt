Observation W verse Additional notes
brothers and sisters who Rom 10:1 remember Paul's audience (both Jew and Gentile Christian) have not met him yet, still he confers relation to them here
my heart's desire / prayer what Rom 10:1 there is no way of misconstruing the point of Paul's words, he desires for his people, the Jews, to receive salvation in Christ
may be saved what Rom 10:1 I like the NIV's translation in the future tense of "may be saved" as this is both Paul's hope, and the future plan in Revelation
I can testify about them what Rom 10:2 the word Paul uses here is the same root from which we get the English word "martyr" meaning to be a credible witness
they are zealous for God what Rom 10:2 the description of zeal is not listed as a negative, but is also not "enough" by itself as his next point states
zeal is not based on knowledge what Rom 10:2 here Paul illustrates that "zeal" or passion for God is not enough by itself, what knowledge our passion is rooted in matters
- did not know righteousness what Rom 10:3 here Paul expands his previous statement about the root of their zeal, it lacked knowledge of righteousness in Christ
- establish on their own what Rom 10:3 because the Jews did not understand righteousness in Christ, they tried to accomplish it on their own
- did not submit what Rom 10:3 notice Paul views the Jews legalism as a rejection of God's righteousness offered through Christ
Christ is the culmination what Rom 10:4 just as a finish line is the goal and end of a race, Jesus is the goal and end of the Law, in Him the Law finds its fulfillment
Moses wrote about what Rom 10:5 quoting from Levitcus 18:5 which establishes obdience requires living "by the Law" not just one time adherence
righteousness by faith what Rom 10:6-8 Paul uses three sucessive verse quotes from Deuteronomy 30:12-14. the section is about lifestyle obedience to God's Law
Believe & declare what Rom 10:9-10 Paul is not subjugating salvation to spoken professions, as many have taught. The term used here for "Lord" denotes one
who has authority over another, like that of a master to a slave or an officer to a soldier. The confession of submission is not
for salvation directly, but more of obedience. The belief in the heart is the act that justifies (v10). Some have taken the
importance of a 'public confession' to be the evidence of the saving faith in the heart, this is not wrong but caution should
be used when over applying it to a requirement for salvation rather than the product of it.
anyone who believes what Rom 10:11 notice again, Paul has belief (faith) as the sole means in which salvation is attained
there is no difference who Rom 10:12 this should not be taken to excuse the differences between Jew and Gentile historically, but in the light of God's grace, there
is no functional difference in who is able to receive grace from God
everyone who calls… will be what Rom 10:13 those not persuaded to the Calvinist view of salvation will point out this verse as the length of God's love, to save all who call
How can they what Rom 10:14 Paul now opens a series of rhetorical questions intended to show the relationship and importance of preaching the Gospel
- how can one BELIEVE if they have not HEARD
- how can one HEAR if someone is not PREACHING
- how can one PREACH if they are not SENT
this three fold questioning illustrates the importance of sharing the Gospel. This verse has been used to justify missions and
preaching in local churches, but remember the context is Paul speaking of the people who would preach to the Jews
feet of those who… who Rom 10:15 quoting from Isaiah 52:7, Paul is again speaking directly of those who will carry the Gospel message to the Jews
but not all accept what Rom 10:16 this is the plight of all who preach the Gospel, there will always be those who are resistent to the message and reject it
faith comes… hearing… of Christwhat Rom 10:16-17 a clear Biblical example of one mssage that one Savior gave the world. There is no outside standard by which men are saved
did they not hear what Rom 10:18-21it is because of the Jewish rejection of Christ that Gentiles are allowed in the first place. God worked through the rejection
