Observation W verse Additional notes
but now what Rom 3:21 Paul uses the closing argument in v20 to transition from "the law" of God to "the grace" of God
- apart from the Law what Rom 3:21 the Law (according to v20) makes us concious of our sin, but now there is something new we can learn outside of that
- the righteousness of God what Rom 3:21 this "righteousness" can be viewed as the impeccable and unstained nature of God's character, or the loving grace flowing
from that character that offers justification instead of judgment. I (Pastor Bill) see it as a both/and, not either/or
- the Law & the Prophets what Rom 3:21 this is a Jewish way of referring to the entire Old Testament. The importance of this can not be overstated. While Jesus came
to do something that was "outside" of the OT, it was not in conflict but rather fulfillment of it.
given through faith what Rom 3:22 the means by which the purity and justification of God is given is by faith, not by works
- in Christ what Rom 3:22 there is no other name in which salvation can be found or bought. Faith IN CHRIST is the only way
- to all who believe what Rom 3:22 there is another major distinction to be made about the NT Gospel in light of its OT heritage. While the reception of the
outstanding covenants are guaranteed by God's character, given to the descendants of Abraham, the reception of salvaiton
in Christ will be received by all though the means of faith, not relation to Abraham
there is no difference who Rom 3:22 putting a fine point on the pastoral issue of the book of Romans, Paul directly combats the disunity between the Jewish
and Gentile Christians at Rome by illustating the complete lack of difference between them
- for all have sinned what Rom 3:23 the first thing in which all Jews and Gentiles have in common is there status as sinners before a holy God
- all fall short what Rom 3:23 the second thing all Jews and Gentiles have in common is falling short of God's glory by themselves, because we are all sinful
- glory of God what Rom 3:23 all people (Jews & Gentiles) fall short of God's glory. His holiness, purity, love, and grace is not something we can reach or
replicate in and of ourselves. No matter how "good" we think we are, we will always fall short of God's glory
- all are justified what Rom 3:24 just as all people (Jew & Gentile) are sinner, they are ALL also justified or declared "not guilty" by God
- by His grace what Rom 3:24 the Greek word for grace is charis (G5485) means "that which brings joy" and is the same root from which we get our modern
day English word "charity" from. Something that is given, not on the basis of merity, but at the behest of the giver
- through Jesus Christ who Rom 3:24 the implication here is undeniable, God's "charity" is to give redemption to all who ask… through Christ and only Christ
God presented Christ what Rom 3:25 it was God the Father's plan for Christ to be presented as the sacrifice that would purchase attonment
- as a sacrifice of attonement what Rom 3:25 the sacrifice for attonment is the one which pays the debt of blood required by the transgression of sin. (see Rom 6:23)
- shedding of blood what Rom 3:25 the first sin in the garden resulted in the shedding of blood and it has remained that way (Gen 3:21)
demonstrate his righteousness what Rom 3:26 God demonstrates his love and unyielding "charity" for us by intentionally shedding the blood of His Son
because of His forbearance what Rom 3:25 the offering of God's love and grace comes from a place of patience on behalf of God Himself
HAD let sins go unpunished what Rom 3:25 the fact that sins did not get immediately punished in the OT is because God is patient, not because sin is acceptable
just… and justifies what Rom 3:26 God is the only just ruler and judge because He is the only being in the universe who is fully just, thus able to justify
- faith in Jesus what Rom 3:26 again, Paul brings to the focus the fact that justification, being declared "not guilty" of sin, can only be found in Jesus
where then is boasting? what Rom 3:27 while this question may be focused on the Jewish audience, boasting in their relationship with Abraham, it could easily also
be applied to the Gentile Christians seeing themselves as the culmination of the "old ways" and boasting about that. Either wQay
the point of the verse is clear, if justification is a gift of grace from God, than nothing is left to boast from the human side
justified by faith what Rom 3:27 for the fourth time in this small section, Paul hammers home the point of faith in Christ being the source of justification
- apart from the Law what Rom 3:28 Paul repeats the point found in v21 that justification is not found in any works of the Law
- law of works / faith what Rom 3:28 here we see a dichotomory between the OT Law that required physical works and the NT Law that requires faith
God of Jews & Gentiles who Rom 3:29 again, the pastoral purpose is found in the uniting of the two "sides" of the Church in Christ
justify by the same faith what Rom 3:29 if there were any possible misunderstanding on how exactly God's gift of grace is muted on the Jewish and/or Gentile sides
of the Church, Paul makes it abundantly clear, the same faith is expressed in Christ from both Jews and Gentiles
nullify vs fulfill the Law what Rom 3:31 this final comment most likely is a premtive comment on a Jewish objection about the gift of grace, the offering of grace in
Christ does not cancel out the OT Scriptures, but rather fulfills their purpose in illusrating the One who gives justification
