Observation W verse Additional notes
love what Rom 12:9 the word used here is "agape" or an all encompassing love
must be sincere what Rom 12:9 sincere love is love that is true and without hypocrisy, this is the kind of love all Christians should display
hate evil what Rom 12:9 the relationship between the believer and anything evil should be hate, we should not want to engage in anything evil
cling to what is good what Rom 12:9 this is the opposite, but not necessarily equal action of the hating of evil, we must strive to cling / hold onto to the good
be devoted to one another what Rom 12:10 the type of "devotion" cited here is the kind of love that exists between married couples and families, the devotion is an
outpouring of the relational love between the people themselves
- in love what Rom 12:10 interestingly, the word Paul uses here is "philadelphia" or brotherly love, as the "devotion" from the previous line states
Honor one another what Rom 12:10 to honor someone is to afix or attach value and importance to them in relation to you
- above yourselves who Rom 12:10 we are called to attach value to others that is above our own determined value of ourselves
never be lacking in zeal what Rom 12:11 being zealous or diligent in our faith is giving "it all" everytime, we are told to never be lacking in "giving it all" to God
- keep your spiritual fervor what Rom 12:11 the imagery here is of keeping something hot or boiling, the meaning to keep the "fire" of our faith "hot" at all times
this can also be translated, "fervent in the Spirit" which could refer to keeping the "fire" of our filling of the Holy Spirit "hot"
- serve the Lord what Rom 12:11 the relationship between the serving and the "fire" of faith here is a bit unclear, either it can mean (a) we should keep the fire
of our faith hot SO THAT we can serve God, or (b) we keep the fire hot BY SERVING, either way the relationship beween the
temperature of our faith and our service to the Lord are linked. Which way do you think is more appropriate?
rejoicing in hope what Rom 12:12 the future hope of the church is the return of Christ and His Kingdom, this hope should always be a cause for praise and worship
persevering in tribulation what Rom 12:12 the Christian life will NOT be one that is easy and carefree. We are to expect trouble, but rejoice in it because of Christ
devoted to prayer what Rom 12:12 another key element to the Christian life is the devotion to prayer, or direct communication with God
contributing to the needs what Rom 12:13 notice all Christians were expected to be a partner or to share in meeting the needs of the family of God
practicing hospitality what Rom 12:13 hospitality is literallly, "to show love to strangers" we are called as Christians to love those we do not know yet
bless those who persecute you what Rom 12:14 to respond to harm with blessing is one of the most challenging activities for believers, but we are following Christ's example
bless and do not curse what Rom 12:14 we are not to be the people of anger, strife, violence, or vengence, we are called by Christ to be a people of blessing
rejoice / mourn with those what Rom 12:15 our relationship with others should be to be "present" with them in all that they are dealing with, not outside looking in
living in harmony with who Rom 12:16 to live in harmony with someone is to have a "same mind" as them, to be thinking the same way
do not be proud what Rom 12:16 pride would be a direct stumbling black to living "in harmony" with everyone, pride often leads us to bigger problems
do not repay evil for evil what Rom 12:17 the desire to "strike back" when we are wronged is powerful, but it is not what God wants for His children
be careful to do what is right what Rom 12:17 we are to always evaluate our actoins to determine if we are doing things "right" or "correctly" but the standard we are to
judge and assess our actions on is not our own scale, but eyes of the collective church
If it is possible, live at peace what Rom 12:18 this leaves open the possibility that being "at peace" with everyone may NOT always be a possibility
do not take revenge what Rom 12:19 Paul has already ruled this out as God is judge (not us) and we are live at peace as much as possible, revenge is thereby forbidden
leave room for God's wrath what Rom 12:19 God sees all the injustice that happens to us and around us. Just because we do not prosecute the wrongs done to us does not
mean they go unrecorded and unanswered. We must "leave room" for God to do His job as the ultimate judge.
heap burning coals what Rom 12:20 the meaning of this phrase is unclear, but what it can't mean, is anyting retalitory in light of the other verses in this section
overcome evil with good what Rom 12:21 the purpose of this section is to illustrate that good must overcome evil in the life of the believer at all times
