LEARN: What Happens When We Die?
A: 6 Views of Death & Afterlife
Materialism: Human persons cease to exist at death; no soul or self survives.
Paganism: The human soul continues in a spiritual realm (e.g., Valhalla, the
Underworld, Summerland).
Absorptionism: The human soul is absorbed into an ultimate or divine reality,
losing individual distinction.
Reincarnation: The human soul undergoes repeated rebirths across multiple
lives.
Eternal Judgment: The human soul is judged after death and assigned an
eternal destiny.
Resurrection Judgment: The human soul is given a new body and judged by
God for eternal destiny.
Christians believe in RESURRECTION JUDGMENT
To understand Christian teaching on what happens after death, you have to
understand the relationship between the SOUL and the BODY.
Maturity Cycle Page 17

B: Understanding Death as Separation
Humans are two-part beings, soul and body. What we call "death" is the
separation of the soul from the body.
Genesis 2:7
7 Then the LORD God formed a man from the dust of the ground and breathed
into his nostrils the breath of life, and the man became a living being.
Ecclesiastes 12:7
7 and the dust returns to the ground it came from, and the spirit returns to
God who gave it.
C: 3 Stages of Human Existence
1: Mortal Stage (Soul + Mortal Body)
In the mortal stage, humans live as embodied souls—soul and body united,
yet subject to weakness and death.
2: Disembodied Stage (Soul without a Body)
In the disembodied stage, humans are alive and conscious with God, yet
incomplete, because the soul exists without a body.
3: Resurrected Stage (Soul + Immortal Body)
In the resurrected stage, humans are made whole again—soul and body
reunited, transformed, and no longer subject to death.
