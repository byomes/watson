this is also why what Rom 13:6 Paul uses the authority of governing officials coming from God to make his next point about taxes
you pay taxes what Rom 13: 6 the paying of money in the form of taxes in order that others may govern was a common practice in both the ancient world
as well as today. This verse is not a comment on how taxes are used, or whether we need to agree with them or not
it is merely pointing out that because those who govern devote their time to it, they are allowed to collect taxes
Give to everyone what you owe who Rom 13:7 Paul expects that those who represent the Savior Messiah will not be in unpaid dept to anyone
taxes/revenue & respect/honor what Rom 13:7 notice Paul does not make a distinction between money owed to an authority in form of taxes, money owed to a vendor
or buisness in the form of revenue, respect (literally fear) to those who should be respected, and honor (literally value)
to those who should be honored. Paul views all of these as "debts" believers can and may "owe" to others
let no debt remain outstanding wha Rom 13:8 in addition to the previous verse, Paul now adds that believers should allow no debt to be outstanding, this also applies
to the giving honor and respect to those whom it is due (whther we agree with them or not)
continuing debt of love what Rom 13:8 the one debt for which believers will never be able to pay is the debt to love one another. This implies that we "owe" one
another our love, not get to chose whether someone else in the body of Christ is worthy of our respect / love
fulfills the law what Rom 13:8 Paul now gives additional reasons why we are to love one another, because we also fulfill the Law of God
Commandments what Rom 13:9 Do not commit adultery (#7), Do not murder (#6), Do not steal (#8), Do not covet (#10) to which Paul adds "whatever
command there may be" which likely includes the rest of the divine commands on how we are to treat each other.
"Love your neighbor as yourself" is the famous teaching that sparked the parable of the Good Samaritan
Love does not harm what Rom 13:10 you cannot love someone and do them harm at the same time. This is impossible.
- to a neighbor who Rom 13:10 Jesus answered the "who is my neighbor" question with the parable of the Good Samaritan. It is anyone we may know, see
or come across that is in need. This command is thus universal for all people, in all times, in all locations.
love is the fulfillment of the Lawwhat Rom 13:10 if love is the fulfillment of the Law, and Jesus came to show the Love of God, than God's love is the fulfillment of His Law

hour has already come when Rom 13:11 the "hour" refers not to a specific hour, but a specific time
- wake from your slumber what Rom 13:11 the "slumber" is not literal sleep, but a time when the readers were unaware of things, like when one is asleep
- because salvation is nearer what Rom 13:11 with each passing breath, we all grow closer to the time when we will be reunited with Christ and this life will be over
