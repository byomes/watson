Esau (aka EDOM)  | who | Gen 36:1 | the elder son of Isaac, brother of Jacob, NOT the brother of promise, father of the Edomites 
 |  |  | Taken as a whole, this chapter tells us what happens to Esau's descedants. Esau becomes the "father" of the Edomites
 |  |  | the progression also shows us the growth from an extended family, to tribal organization, to monarchy 
 |  |  | we see Esau has a 12 tribe or clan unit which is in keeping with the famlies of Nahor, Ishmael, and Jacob
 |  |  | The Edomites show us again and again in the Bible, so they are not out of the picture, but are not the focus of God's story
 |  |  | The King of Edom refuses the Israelites to go through his land after the Exodus - Numbers 20:18
 |  |  | The Israelites are instructed to not hate the Edomites - Deuteronomy 23:7
 |  |  | An Edomites was King Saul's chief herdsmen - 1 Samuel 21:7
 |  |  | An Edomites killed 85 Israelites priests - 1 Samuel 22:18
 |  |  | King David subjugated the Edomites - 2 Samuel 8:14
 |  |  | Hadad the Edomite was King Solomon's adversary - 1 Kings 11:14
 |  |  | Edomites revolt against Judah and crown their own king - 2 Kings 8:20
 |  |  | The Amalekites - are a significant enemy for the Israelites in the Old Testament, here is where they come from
 |  |  | The Moses "arms up" battle was with the Amakelites - Exodus 17
