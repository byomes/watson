Observation W verse Additional notes
brothers and sisters who Rom 7:1 the root word here is "adelphos" which means child of the same parents, or belonging to the same people group
whose who know the law who Rom 7:1 Paul is speaking to the Jewish Christians at Rome, but the spiritual application for his teaching applies equally to Gentiles
the Law what Rom 7:1 the law that Paul is speaking aboout is the Torah, the Law of God
- has authority what Rom 7:1 the Law of God has authority over us because it is from God, when the Creator makes a law, it governs all creation
- as long as… person lives when Rom 7:1 the Law of God has authority over us until we die, because the Law is given to guide THIS life leading us to the next
For example what Rom 7:2 Paul now gives an example, this passage should not be used to try and establish Paul's view on marriage and/or divorce
- woman… to her husband what Rom 7:2 this is a Jewish view of marriage as only men could seek divorce, in Roman culture either party could seek a divorce
- if her husband dies what Rom 7:2 if the husband is the object or person to whom she is bound in marriage, in this example, he is the "root" she is bound to
- released from the law what Rom 7:2 because the object of the bondage is gone, the now widow is relased from the terms of the bondage, ie marriage
Example of the example what Rom 7:3 Paul now uses the example of sexual activity of the ficticious woman in his example. There are two options
Option A - the woman has sexual relations with another man while still married, this is breaking the law and is adultery
Option B - the woman marries another man and has sexual relations with the second husband, this not breaking the law
* notice that the sexual contact with another man is ONLY allowable inside the bounds of a new marriage
so my brothers and sistes what Rom 7:4 Paul now stands on the analogy of the widow to illustrate the relationship between Jewish Christians and the Law of God
you also died to the law what Rom 7:4 the quesiton left in Jewish Christians minds after the end of chapter 6 is, "what is our relationship to the Law then?" Paul's
answer is spelled out here in chapters 7 and 8, they have "died" to the Law, in the analogy then are graduated out of it
- through the body of Christ where Rom 7:4 obviously the Jewish believers didn't all physically die, but through the death of Christ, the "binding" to the Law died
- belong to another who Rom 7:4 as in the widow analogy, the husband died and the woman was able to marry (or be bound) to another man. This is the way
Paul is speaking of Jewish believers. They were "bound" to the Law (1st husband), and are now "bound" to Christ (2nd husband)
- to Him who was raised who Rom 7:4 Paul here identifies the "new husband" that believers are now "bound" to, namely Jesus. Just as in the previous chapter,
everyone is either a slave to sin or to God, there is no middle ground. Here, the "new husband" is to whom we are bound
- that we might bear fruitwhat Rom 7:4 what was the reason that Christ died, was raised, and has "purchased" us as His new bride? So we can bear fruit
when we were in... the flesh when Rom 7:5 the "realm of the flesh" is a way of saying, bound to the "first husband" or "slave" to the first master, to sin
- sinful passions… worked in uswhat Rom 7:5 while we were "owned" by sin, the "work" we did was sin, this was aroused by a passion or desire for sin
- we bore fruit for death what Rom 7:5 the fruit, or product of our work, while "owned" or married to sin, was we brought death to ourselves
but now when Rom 7:6 there is a definitive moment of change, just as the widow is no longer a widow but a newly married woman, a binary change
by dying to what once bound uswhat Rom 7:6 the "death" we "die" in through Christ, not in and of ourselves
- we have been released what Rom 7:6 we are now released from the first "master" or "husband" ie, released from bondage to sin
- from the law what Rom 7:6 this is another big hint that Paul's intended audience is Jewish Christians here, as Gentile believers aren't boudn to the law
- so that we may serve what Rom 7:6 we are relased from the law for the purpose of service in the Spirit, again, there is no middle ground
is the law sinful? …. No what Rom 7:7 preparing for a possible heretical spin on his words, Paul ensures to address a misunderstanding that could twist his words
would not have known sin what Rom 7:7 we are able to know much more about sin, because the Law tells us what sin is, ie what is disobedient to God
sin, seizing the opportunity what Rom 7:8 sin is personified here as an active agent, taking advantage of the knowledge the law brings to tempt us to sin all the more
apart from the law, sin was deadwhat Rom 7:8 Paul is not saying the law created sin, he is illustrating that when more detail is given about God's will, one knows more sin
life, death, and the law what Rom 7:9-10 Paul is speaking of the natural human experience, not only his own. The more we know of God's Word, the more we know of
His expectations of us. The more we know of His will, the more we are able to know what sin is, and thus sin "more"
seizing the opportunity what Rom 7:11 Paul repeats the same point he made in verse 8, because the Law spelled out sin in greater detail, we know more about sin
the law is holy and good what Rom 7:12 the law of God is good because God is good, we only know more about sin and evil because it is the opposite of good
did the good bring about death what Rom 7:13 Paul's quesiton here is if the good law brings about death? No, it does not. The good law does not cause us to disobey it.
