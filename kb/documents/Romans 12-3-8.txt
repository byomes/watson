Observation W verse Additional notes
by the grace given me what Rom 12:3 Paul makes a passionate appeal to his readers "by the grace of God" or in accordance with the calling to serve as an apostle
I say who Rom 12:3 Paul makes this appeal directly, as the apostle, to the Church at Rome
- to every one of you who Rom 12:3 this includes both "halves" of the Roman church, the Jews and the Gentiles
think of yourself more highly what Rom 12:3 the sin of pride always begins as a thought of one's self in comparison or constrast to another, believers are to avoid this
- than you ought what Rom 12:3 interestingly, Paul leaves the responsibility in place to see one's self as we "ought" or "need to" this also rules out false
humility in a vain effort to see one's self in any other light other than the truth
- but rather what Rom 12:3 instead of thinking of one's self in a way that is more high (or low) than one "need to"
- sober judgment what Rom 12:3 sober or sound judgment is one that comes from being in control of or in one's "right mind" ie, truthful or in reality
- accordance with the faith what Rom 12:3 Christians are to judge themselves with their faith, not in the absense of it
- distributed to each of you what Rom 12:3 the "faith" each of us has is given to us as a gift from God, we all should not expect to have the same measure of faith
for just as what Rom 12:4 Paul here begins the "body of Christ" analogy, it is intended to explain the point form the previous verse
one body with many members what Rom 12:4 then and now, we naturally understand that our bodies have different "parts" that help comprise the "whole" of ourselves
- do not have the same functionwhat Rom 12:4 the various parts of the body all perform different functions, this would make the "judgment by comparison" of one body
part to another (the focus of verse 3) rather difficult as they are all purposed to do very different things
- so in Christ what Rom 12:4 Paul uses the physical analogy of the human body to show the Jewish and Gentile Roman Christians how they are related
- we, though many who Rom 12:5 here the pastoral purpose of the book of Romans really shines through the text, Paul sees himself as part of the Church even
though, at the time of the writing of the letter, he has not been there yet.
- form one body what Rom 12:5 the natural working together of differing "parts" in the Church should be the same as in the human body
- each member belongs what Rom 12:5 Paul doubles down on his analogy here, pointing out that each "part" of the body of Christ no longer belongs to itself
- to all the others who Rom 12:5 the mutual "belonging" of the body is vital to its working together. If the stomach didn't understand that it "belonged" to the
rest of the body, the loss of that function would be felt systemically throughout the whole body. We, as Chrisitans, are to
understand our relationship to each other in this way as well. We truly need one another to be fully healthy and capable
we have different gifts what Rom 12:6 like the "parts" of the human body, the different members in the Body of Christ have unique and varrying gifts
- according to the grace what Rom 12:6 just as Paul began his appeal with the grace given to him (see verse 3), here too the other members of the Body are understood
to have different gifts given to them by the grace of God
- gift of prophesying what Rom 12:6 the technical definition of prophecy is to "speech eminating from divine inspiration" this can be telling the future like the Old
testament prophets, but it can also be teaching, correcting, comforting, or anything else the Spirit might lead one to say.
- in accordance with your faithwhat Rom 12:6 the only instruction given to those who could "prophesy" is to do so in line with one's faith, not apart from it
- if it is to serve, then serve what Rom 12:7 the root word here means to "wait on tables" and implies physical service for, to, or of others
- if it is teaching, then teach what Rom 12:7 one who teaches or instructs in that which is right ie, doctrine, learning, instruction
- if it is to encourage, than… what Rom 12:8 the verb here carries the picture of calling or raising someone else up to your side, to "help them up" through words
- if it is giving, give generously what Rom 12:8 literally, to impart or to physically give away, the intended implication here is to give away something that is yours to start
- if to lead, do it diligently what Rom 12:8 to "lead" in this context is to have authority over another for the purpose of responsibility, to be set over or to rule over
- if to show mercy… cheerfully what Rom 12:8 to show mercy here is to help someone who is afflicted or in worse shape than you are expected
