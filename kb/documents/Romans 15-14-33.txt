Observation W verse Additional notes
I myself who Rom 15:14 Paul addresses himself writing first person to an audience who hadn't met him yet, but likely had heard about him
brothers and sisters who Rom 15:14 adelphos' a term for the collective group of Roman Christians, both Jewish and Gentile
- full of goodness what Rom 15:14 the intention of the word here is not only 'doing good things' but more the sense of preferring morally right over wrong
- filled with knowledge what Rom 15:14 the word used here is 'gnosis' and refers to intellectual knoweldge
- competent to instruct what Rom 15:14 Paul's final compliment to the Roman Christians is the culmination of the previous two, they are both desiring moral
goodness and knowledgable enough to do so, born from this is the desire to instruct, or teach to avoid wrongdoing
yet I have written to you what Rom 15:15 Paul recognizes he uses forceful language to write to the Roman church, not for the purpose of judgment, but of encouragement
- to remind you what Rom 15:15 the goal of the apostle in the letter to the Romans is to "remind" them of that that which he believes they "know"
- because of the grace what Rom 15:15 Paul's credentials for writing this letter is because of the grace ('charis') of God, not his own authority or any other human
- to be a minister what Rom 15:15 the word here is literally 'one who cares for the affairs of another' ie, a servant in service of another who is greater
- of Christ Jesus who Rom 15:16 there is no doubt what 'master' Paul is serving, he is the minister (servant) of the Jesus Christ alone
- to the Gentile who Rom 15:16 Paul's calling to serve the Lord with His affairs is the specific calling to that of the Gentiles (a large group in the Roman church)
priestly duty of proclaiming what Rom 15:16 in Paul's view, the privilege of proclaiming the Gospel was like that of a priest who served God in the Temple
- so that the Gentiles who Rom 15:16 Paul again states his purpose to be the Apostle to the Gentiles
- become and offering what Rom 15:16 the goal is not merely to "grow" the church, but to prepare the hearts and lives of the Gentiles to be acceptable (holy) to God
- sanctified… the Holy Spirit who Rom 15:16 the ultimate goal of a convert is to be sanctified, which is to be consecrated, holy, and dedicated to God
Therefore what Rom 15:17 remember to ask, "what is the therefore there for?" this is a conclusion based on the previous calling
I glory in Christ in my service what Rom 15: 17 unlike the old pharisee, Paul now finds his glory in Christ, and not the adherence to the Law from Moses, his passion,
dedicated, and service to God however have not changed. He is still as passionate, but now for Christ, not legalism
except what Christ has what Rom 15:18 though Paul may arguably be the most influencial apostle of the first century, he did not see his own accomplishments as
anything other than what Christ has done through him
- leading Gentiles to obey what Rom 15:18 Paul's calling was to reach the Gentile world with the message of Christ to lead them to obey God, not just be 'saved'
- by signs and wonders what Rom 15:19 the supernatural power of the Spirit was a powerful witness to the calling and work of God in Paul's life and ministry
From Jerusalem to Illyricum where Rom 15:19 Jerusalem is the center of Jewish life, on the eastern side of the Mediterrian Sea, Illyricum is on the north eastern side of
the Adriatic sea, across from Rome itself, this area is modern day Croatia and Bosnia/Herzegovina
always been my ambition what Rom 15:20 Paul now begins to speak of his heart and and his desire for the next season of his ministry, taking the gospel to more
Gentile nations that have yet to hear the Gospel
as it is written what Rom 15:21 Paul justifies his desire to preach where the Gospel has not been yet by quoting the prophet Isaiah 52:15
prevented from coming to you what Rom 15:22-23this is likely not a reference to spiritual warfare, but rather the amount of work Paul needed to do in the area before leaving
when I visit Spain where Rom 15:24 here Paul reveals his plans to travel through Rome on his way to carry the Gospel to Spain
have you assist me what Rom 15:24 not shy after all that he has shared, Paul is making a direct request of the Roman christians to help him minister in Spain
on my way to Jerusalem what Rom 15:25-27in Acts 24:17 we learn that Paul collected an offering on his third missionary journey to bring to the Jerusalem church
join in my struggle by praying what Rom 15:30 Paul considered prayers a part of "joining" him in the struggle for the ministry of the Gospel
- kept safe what Rom 15:31 the first request Paul makes is to be kept safe on the journey to Jerusalem
- contribution received what Rom 15:31 Paul was transporting a large sum of money to help the church in Jerusalem, the money has a purpose for ministry
come to you with joy what Rom 15:32 Paul's desire is to "run well" the task of delivering the money and then head to visit Rome to be "refreshed" before Spain
