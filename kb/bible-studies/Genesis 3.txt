Observation | W | verse | Additional notes
the serpent | what | Gen 3:1 | a snake, the real animal not a symbol 
asked the woman | what | Gen 3:1 | the fact that the snake spoke illustrates that it was being used under Satan's power as snakes don't talk normally 
the woman (Eve)  | who | Gen 3:1 | Eve, the first female, created by God from Adam's rib the first wife
Did God really say…? | what | Gen 3:1 | the beginning of all temptation comes with quesitoning God's word to undo our trust in it 
not eat from any tree | what | Gen 3:1 | Satan's quesiton was a lie, and reframed God's law as more restrictive than it was in the first place
of course we may eat | what | Gen 3:2 | Eve correctly recounts God's original plan, the freedom to eat of ALL with the restriction of only ONE
it's only the fruit of the tree | what | Gen 3:2 | Eve had not yet been created when God gave this law to Adam, this illustrates that Adam taught Eve about God's law
must not eat it / touch it | what | Gen 3:3 | God's restriction wasn't only on the eating, but they were not to touch it either, avoiding temptation is an all or nothing deal
you will die | what | Gen 3:3 | the wages of sin is death (Romans 6:23) from the beginning disobeying God's law brought death
you won't die | what | Gen 3:4 | again, Satan tries to get the first couple to question God's word, this time by questioning His response to disobedience
God knows your eyes will… | what | Gen 3:5 | Satan frames the reason for God's law as petty positioning, ignoring the blissful state that God created for Adam & Eve
you will be like God | what | Gen 3:5 | just as Satan fell to the same temptation, he now tempts humanity with the chance to play God - which we are still doing
knowing both good and evil | what | Gen 3:5 | it wasn't until the fall that mankind had knowledge of evil, until that point we were literally "perfect and happy" 
saw… beautiful & delicious | what | Gen 3:6 | the tree was made that way when God planted the garden (Gen 2:9)  Satan distorts the truth of God in order to tempt us to sin
3 "categories" of temptation | what | Gen 3:6 | Eve was tempted mentally (looked good), physically, (delicious), emotionally, (wanted the wisdom) 
who was WITH HER | what | Gen 3:6 | many have tried to say that Eve was cornered or somehow separated from Adam and thus sinned "first" this is not true
their eyes were opened | what | Gen 3:7 | interesting that the sense most acute to determine good and evil is sight.  Pornography: I know it when I see it
suddenly felt shame | what | Gen 3:7 | until this point there was no shame in their marriage, now because of sin, shame found it's way between husband & wife
at their nakedness | what | Gen 3:7 | this is not only the state of being unclothed, but now also the understanding that they are "exposed" and guilty of sin 
sewed fig leaves together | what | Gen 3:7 | man always tries to "cover up" our own sin by our own means which are weak and ill-fated from the beginning
walking in the garden | what | Gen 3:8 | the fact that God is "walking" implies that He has taken human form, logically, God the Son is the most likely choice
they hid from God | what | Gen 3:8 | once sin entered the picture, spiritual death destroyed their relationship with God, so much that they hid from His presence 
where are you?  | what | Gen 3:9 | God being omniscient didn't need to ask, but this shows even in our sin, how much God wants to talk with us

the woman you gave me… | what | Gen 3:12 | the "blame game" begins as humanity tries to pass of the responsibility for sin to a third party
the serpent decieves me… | what | Gen 3:13 | Eve follows Adam's example and tries to blame the serpent for her actions
God the Father | what | Gen 3:8-11 | one thing that is clear from God's words and actions is His desire to be a Father and not just Creator or Judge
