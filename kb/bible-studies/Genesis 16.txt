Abram | who | Gen 16:1 | Soon to be called Abraham, Progenator of the nation of Israel, first man to be called a Hebrew (Gen 14:13)
barren / unable to have child | what | Gen 16:1 | the state of Sarai's womb is also the state of God's promises. "barren" judges only the past, The Lord promises the future

the Lord is preventing me… | what | Gen 16:2 | this shows an immaturity in Sarai's faith, the Lord never promises that which He won't fulfill, He just hadn't yet

have children through her | what | Gen 16:2 | like the Fall in Eden, Abram is tempted to and does sin based on his wife's inclination

where: coming & going | what | Gen 16:8 | like Jesus with the Samaritan woman at the well, He gives Hagar the opportunity to be honest with Himself
more descendants | what | Gen 16:10 | The Lord promises Hagar that she too will have many descendants, this is not an extension of the Abrahamic Covanent
Ishmael: God Hears | what | Gen 16:11 | even in the midst of the sin of adultery, the Lord still dealt patiently and calmly with Hagar

You are the God who sees | what | Gen 16:13 | it is clear by her language, Hagar understood that she was talking to the Lord, not a representative
86 years old | when | Gen 16:15 | the age of Abram when Ishmael was born.
