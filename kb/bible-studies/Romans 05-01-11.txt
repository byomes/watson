Observation W verse Additional notes
Therefore what Rom 5:1 Paul transitions from the points made in the previous chapter to a point which stands on their foundation
since we have been justified what Rom 5:1 BECAUSE we have been justified… the point Paul makes is BECAUSE we have been justified
- through faith what Rom 5:1 Paul makes it clear again, we are justified by faith, not by our own works of rigtheousness or legalism
- we have peace with God what Rom 5:1 one of the "effects" caused by our justification by faith is that we now have peace with God our Creator
- through Jesus Christ what Rom 5:1 because we have faith in Christ, Christ gives us peace in our relationship with God
through Christ… gained access what Rom 5:2 Paul also makes it abundantly clear that it is through Christ we are not only granted peace, but that it is offered by grace
which we now stand when Rom 5:2 there is no "waiting period" to the justification that brings peace. It is immediately granted, hence "we now stand"
we boast what Rom 5:2 the word Paul uses here can also be translated: rejoice, glory, extol. The main idea being the verbal declaration of hope
- hope of the glory of God what Rom 5:2 the hope of the church is the two-fold glorious future (a) Jesus is returning, (b) we will spend eternity with Him
not only so… current suffering what Rom 5:3 not only are we to glory, boast, rejoice, extol etc in our future hope, but also in our current sufferings
suffering produces persereveranwcehat Rom 5:3 like James, Paul directly connects "suffering" or "trial" with the purpose for it in our lives, to produce fruit.
- perserverance > character what Rom 5:4 once we have endured "suffering/trial" we will then develop character. This is the character of Christ, not solely human ideas
- character > hope what Rom 5:4 once our character has taken root and become a part of who we are, the byproduct of this is hope
hope does not put us to shame what Rom 5:5 in anticipation of a counter argument, Paul illustrates that posessing hope is not a sign of weakness or shame
- God love… in our hearts where Rom 5:5 the love of God is not just an idea, but a real thing that is "poured" in our hearts. It also works out in our character and hope
- through the Holy Spirit who Rom 5:5 it is the ministry of the Holy Spirit to be our comforter and guide, He ministers to the deep recesses of our hearts
at the just time when Rom 5:6 God's gracious decision to offer salvation to mankind it also not an abstract idea, but one that happened in time
- while we were sinneres who Rom 5:6 when Jesus came and when He died, we were still lost sinners. There is no part of man that makes us worthy of His love
- Christ died what Rom 5:6 as the previous chapters make clear, Christ died for humanity purchasing justification for us by his death
- for the ungodly who Rom 5:6 Christ didn't just die for those who are on the inside of the "jewish" club, but for all people who were ungodly sinners
rarely… but maybe what Rom 5:7 Paul makes an analogy between God's sacrifice and what a human person might do. While someone "may" choose to die
for a good person, no one would ever willingly choose to die for an evil or ungodly person
God demonstrates His love what Rom 5:8 God's love is also not an idea or a trivial platitude, God's love is now fully demonstrated by Christ
while we were yet sinners who Rom 5:8 notice Paul includes himself in the group here, as "we" were yet sinners. Paul himself is also a sinner not deserving God's love
Christ died for us what Rom 5:8 God's love is demonstrated by the sacrifice of Christ. The ultimate price demonstrates the ultimate love
since we have been justified what Rom 5:9 Paul reprises the same argument from verse one of this chapter, BECAUSE we have been justified, not justified ourselves
by His blood what Rom 5:9 because sin required a death payment, Christ's blood was shed and His death is applied to us in justification
saved from God's wrath what Rom 5:9 because we have been saved by Christ's blood and justified, we no longer have the coming wrath or judgment of God to fear
saved through His life what Rom 5:10 Paul points out that while Christ's death bought our justification with God, the story doesn't stop there. Jesus is alive!
not only is this so what Rom 5:11 not only are we saved by His death and life….
we also boast in God what Rom 5:11 because of all that Paul has reviewed, our reponse is to boast, rejoice, glory, and/or extol God
we have… reconciliation what Rom 5:11 in order to be reconciled, there must have been a previous relationship, through Christ, we are returned to a right relationship
with God, one in which we are His righteous children, still responsible to live in obedience. Q
