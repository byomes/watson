the wrath of God what Rom 1:18 the just and active working of God motivated by His righteous judgment on sin and wickedness
- from heaven where Rom 1:18 Heaven, the abode of God, the place of righteousness and peace
- against people's wickedness what Rom 1:18 God's righteous action (his wrath) is motivated by our unrighteous actions (our wickedness)
- supress the truth what Rom 1:18 wicked actions work to supress the truth of God's revelation
God has made it plain to them what Rom 1:19 God has plainly revealed Himself in His creation. This is called "Natural Revelation" God showing Himself naturally in creation
- since creation when Rom 1:19 the natural revelation of God's existence have been in place since He made all that is in existence today
- God's invisible qualitites what Rom 1:19 through natural revelation, God has revealed two aspects of Himself:
- God's eternal power what Rom 1:19 even without Special Revelation (Prophecy and Scripture), the size and composition of the universe cries of a power creator
- God's divine nature what Rom 1:19 in order for a natural creation to come into existence, a SUPERnatural cause is required, this is the Cosmological Argument
- clearly seen what Rom 1:20 Paul states that natural theology is "plainly seen" thus the evidence that God exists is undeniable. The only way to end
at the conclusion that God does not exist is to intentionally supress or replace the truth with a lie
- are without excuse what Rom 1:20 because of the natural revelation God has left in His creation, humanity is guilty of ignoring or disobeying Him
For although they knew God who Rom 1:21 Paul now focuses on the sinful and wicked people who suppress the truth of God
- neither glorified what Rom 1:21 in our wickedness, we are drawn to glorify other things that further our sin, thus we do not glorify God as we should
- nor gave thanks what Rom 1:21 in our wickedness, we are no longer thankful to the Lord for His many blessings, this is another sign of a wicked life
- thinking became futile what Rom 1:21 the word here carries the connotations of "empty" and "self absorbed or vain" sinful minds are selfish minds
- hearts were darkened what Rom 1:21 in wickedness, the heart is "turned" or focused on the things of the dark, evil, sinful, and devoid of light
- claim to be wise what Rom 1:22 the claim of wisdom and understanding is indicative of one who is convinced of his own superiority
- became fools what Rom 1:22 if "the fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom" (Prov 9:10), then the rejection of Him is the beginning of foolishness
- exchanged the glory what Rom 1:23 the illustration of the "foolishness" in the wicked heart is the replacement of God (and His glory) with anything terrestrial
- therefore God gave them what Rom 1:24 God's judgement is to allow humans to chase the depths of their own depravity. This can be likened to a child playing in the
mud in white clothes. At first the allure of playing draws them, the stain of the activity does not even phase them. Eventually
when you're entire outfit is so covered and colored by the mud, and all those you surround yourself with are the same, you
no longer remember or care that you were meant to be clean and presentable. The mud becomes the only thing that matters.
God does not do this to "loose" us to the sin we desire, but for us to see the wretched depravity of it and repent.
- sinful desires of the heart what Rom 1:24 the desire to sin begins in the heart. It comes from a "darkened" heart that rejects God and desires wickedness instead
- degrading their bodies what Rom 1:24 the act of sin degrades the body the commits it. Humans have been degrading themselves with sex since the beginning
- exchanged truth for lie what Rom 1:25 God alone is the source of truth. The evidence of human wickedness is the walking away from truth for a lie we like
- worshiped and served what Rom 1:25 the outworking of our acceptance of lie over truth is seen in the things we as humans worship and serve other than God
- created things not Creator what Rom 1:25 worshiping something created rather than the One who created it is the evidience of exchanging the truth
who is forever praised what Rom 1:25 Paul illustrates the only thing worth worshiping in the world is the true God and creator who has revealed Himself in
His creation denoting his power and authority
Because of this (the exchange) what Rom 1:26,28 because of the exchanging of glory for sin and truth for lie, the effects are visible in man's relationship with man
- gave them over to lusts what Rom 1:26 the rampant presence of lust in human culture throughout history is an illustration of God's wrath (v18) on man's sin
- female homosexuality what Rom 1:27 exchanged natural sexual relations. Sex is not bad, it is created by God, but it was created to be between a man and a woman
- male homosexuality what Rom 1:27 men followed the same pattern of being drawn into sexual immorality. God's judgment on sin is on all humanity
- received the penalty what Rom 1:27 the self-destructive nature of sexual sin is not an accident, it is the judgment from God on the sin itself
- become filled with wickednesswhat Rom 1:29-31 Paul offers a long list of examples of wickendess that are examples of God's judgment of allowing man to chase sin

illustrate why humanity needs the Gospel
