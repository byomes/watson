I (Pastor Bill) have marked the three main reasons Paul gives for his writing as 1, 2, and 3.
(1) - to impart to you a gift what Rom 1:11 this doesn't mean a spiritual gift as in Corinthians, because Paul is not the giver of the gifts, this refers to a gift or blessing
that he himself could bring, ie his teaching of the Gospel and doctrine
- to make you strong what Rom 1:11 the function of the "gift" Paul wanted to give the Romans was to strengthen their faith
- mutual encouragement what Rom 1:12 the way in which Paul planned on making them "strong" was to help them strengthen one another
brothers and sisters who Rom 1:13 the recipients of this letter were the "all y'all" body of believers at Rome, not a specific person or select group

- I am not ashamed of what Rom 1:16 Paul begins this verse with "for" meaning I am eager to preach (v15) "because I am not ashamed of the Gospel"
- is the power of God what Rom 1:16 the Gospel message is the power or "transforming force that effects change" ie change in man's relationship with God
- brings salvation what Rom 1:16 that byproduct of the transforming effective force of change in our relationship with God is what brings salvation
- everyone who believes what Rom 1:16 the power of salvation comes only through faith, it is not a work of man or works, but of belief
- first Jew, then Gentile who Rom 1:16 the power of salvation came first TO the Jews, then THROUGH the Jews. God chose the Abraham and His descendants to be

- righteousness of God what Rom 1:17 there are two views of what the "righteousness of God" means. View 1 is the "righteousness" is God's righteousness revealed
in providing salvation to mankind. View 2, the "righteousness of God" is the act of applying His righteousness to our
un-deserving accounts thereby making us righteous not by our own efforts, but by His.
- by faith, from faith what The meaning of this phrase is also debated. The likely meanings are from the Faithfulness of God to the faith of man believing
in Him. It can also mean the faith in Christ as replaced the faith of the Law.
righteous will live by faith what Rom 1:17 the essence of Paul's Gospel message is that we are not "made righteous" or viewed as no longer guilty of our sin because of
any action we ourselves take, but by the faith we place in God for having declared us not guilty by His grace
