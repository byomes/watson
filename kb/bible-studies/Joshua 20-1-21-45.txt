Observation W verse Additional notes
Cities of Refuge what Jos 20:1 the establishment of the cities of refuge happened earlier in the Law, see Numbers 35:9-34, Deuteronomy 4:41-43; 19:1-14
the LORD who Jos 20:1 the covenant name for Joshua, God is still speaking as the covenant maker who chose, delivered, and gifted the promised land
Joshua who Jos 20:1 the chosen, apointed, and anointed leader of the nation of Israel after Moses, both a military leader and prophet for God's people
tell the Israelites who Jos 20:2 the instructions were for the collective (establish the cities) for the benefit of the individuals (cause of accidental death)
refuge what Jos 20:2 the word here literaly means, "to take in" or "to protect" identifying them as places which to seek asylum from revenge
kills a person what Jos 20:3 the Bible makes a clear distinction between premeditated murder and accidental manslaughter (Num 35:9-15; 35:16-21)
accidentally what Jos 20:3 the possibility exists that one could without intention or knowledge be the cause of another's death, this is accidental
unintentionally what Jos 20:3 unintentionally is slighly different than accidenatlly, this verb carries the lack of forethought, not necessarily intention
may flee there where Jos 20:3 absent a larger form of uniting government, the cities of refuge added hope and insured a better process of justice than vengence
avenger of blood who Jos 20:3 this is the person whose family member has been slain and who is seeking revenge
stand at the entrance where Jos 20:4 the entrance to the city gate is where the city buisness and justice was both discussed and determinied
state their case what Jos 20:4 the cities of refuge were not automatic entrance places, a good case must be made, or the avenger of blood will have their revenge
the elders who Jos 20:4 the eldest, and therefore wisest members of the city, a precursor but not exact analgoy to elders leading in the NT
the fugative who Jos 20:4 the use of the word "fugative" in our modern day language sounds like a presumption of guilt, in actuality it is the recognition of their
fleeing or escaping the wrath and vengence of the avenger who is seeking them
provide a place what Jos 20:4 if the seeker of refuge had a legitimate case, the elders would not only protect but also provide for them, this was God's justice in work
must not surrender what Jos 20:5 if and when the vengence is sought, the elders of the refuge cities had a responbility to keep the refugee safe until such time as they
could return to their original home
after they have stood trial what Jos 20:6 the refugee was not free on all accounts, they were kept safe until a trial could be held and true justice sought in the matter
high priests death what Jos 20:6 two theories have been presented as to why the high priest's death is of note in this passage. The first is that as the cities of refuge were
all levitical cities, the high priest's passing was a "change of administration" and thus all refugees were released. The second is like the
first, but relies more on the high priest's role as the one who is responsible to seek forgiveness for the sins of the people. His death
attoned for the sin of the "manslayer" and thus the avenger of blood no longer has a need for vengence. Of the two, I (Pastor Bill) find the
second more reasonable as there is little evidence the high priest's service was viewed as an "administration" at this time in history.

or any foreigner who Jos 20:9 notice this practice of justice applied equally to all of God's chosen people and all those living among them
the Levitical cities where Jos 21:1-41 finally the Levites are given their town among the other tribes of the people. The cities of refuge were all Levitical towns.
the land he had sworn what Jos 21:43 It is here that the people have finally inherited the land that God promised them so many years ago.
LORD gave them rest what Jos 21:44 the imagery of "rest of every side" further develops the power of God to not only provide but also protect His people from outsiders
everyone was fulfilled what Jos 21:45 the final state of the nation of God's people is blessed, provided for, and kept safe by the Lord God, as He always intended it to be
