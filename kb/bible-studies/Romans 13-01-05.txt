Observation W verse Additional notes
Let everyone be subject what Rom 13:1 Paul continues the theme he began back in 12:14 of how the Roman Christians are to relate to those outside the church
- to the governing authority who Rom 13:1 at the time Paul wrote these words, Nero was emperor of Rome! This directive is not a comment on the character or godliness
of the governing authority to which we submit, but a requirement to submit because of our Christian character
no authority except God's what Rom 13:1 God is the first, last, and only authority in the universe, all authority is therefore derived from his
- established by God the classic label of "kingmaker" belongs to God alone, He is the sole authority of the universe and if He has allowed (or placed)
a person in a position of authority it is because it under His authority
Consequently what Rom 13:2 because of the truth of the previous verse, the truth of the second is also true
- whoever rebels who Rom 13:2 Paul here groups all who rebel (or refuse to submit) to an earthly authority
- rebels against God what Rom 13:2 because authority can only be derived from God, it is His authority that is over all human authority, thus to rebel against a
human authority is to also rebel against God's. The only time civil disobedience is thereby permitted is when a human authority
attempts to require an action that is directly contrary to God's. See Acts 4:19
- bring judgment on themselveswhat Rom 13:2 when we rebel against human authority when it is not in direct conflict with God's authority, we knowingly place ourselves
in a place of judgment for our disobedience. This is not persecution, this is choosing judgment.
no terror for those who do rightwhat Rom 13:3 when one obeys the law, there is no reason to fear the law
free from fear… of authority what Rom 13:3 the easy way to be unafraid of authority is to obey, and therefore have no reason to fear the authority itself
the one in authority who Rom 13:4 all earthly authorities, from presidents and kings all the way down to teachers, principles, etc
- is God's servant what Rom 13:4 this does not mean the person in authority is Godly by any means. This means that as one who has been given authority to
exercise, they are operating as a servant of God, and will also answer for the way in which they do
- for your good what Rom 13:4 here Paul reveals the purpose for authority, to be able to provide "good" for those who submit to you, any use of authority
that is not focused on this purpose is sinful and wrong.
bear the sword what Rom 13:5 this is a literal and figurative description of authoritative punishment for disobedience
agents of wrath what Rom 13:4 another purpose for which God has given his authority to earthly rulers is to deliver present wrath for wrong doing, God as the
sole cosmic authority reserves the future wrath for His own distribution, but earthly authorities are given the right and the
responsibility to deliver wrath to lawbreakers, for the purpose of establishing good for people, not instilling fear
Therefore what Rom 13:5 another transitional word, Paul is now going to stand on the points made in verses 1-4 to summate another point
it is necessary what Rom 13:5 something that is necessary is imposed by either circumstances or authority, in this case, I would suggest it is necessary
because of God's authority being given to the human agents
- not only what Rom 13:5 Paul offers two complimentary reasons why it is necessary to submit to all human governing authorities
- possible punishment what Rom 13:5 we are to submit to governing authorities because they have the authority to punish us for disobedience
- matter of conscience what Rom 13:5 we are to submit to earthly human authorities because our conscience before God should lead us to do so
Examples of civil disobedience in the Scriptures:
Hebrew Midwives in Exodus in Exodus 15, the Hebrew midwives are instructed to kill babies and disobey because they feared God more than Pharoah
Saul's soldiers saving Johnathan in 1 Samual 14, Jonahtan violates an instruction he is not aware of, the soliders save Jonathan from unjust punishment
Esther's uninvited entrance in Esther 4, Esther goes before the king uninvited to save her people, God vindicates her faith and her people
The Firery Furnace in Daniel 5, three Hebrew servants refuse to worship the statue of the king and are saved from the firery furnace
Daniel & The Lion's Den in Daniel 6, Daniel is thrown into the lions den after disobeying a restriction on prayer
The Magi & King Herod in Matthew 2, the Magi are warned not to return to Herod and disobey his instruction to return to Him
Peter & John Preaching in Acts 4, Peter and John are told to stop preaching in the name of Jesus, they choose to obey God rather than man
Paul & Silas in Prison in Acts 16, Paul and Silas are beaten and jailed because the ministry they did, their angelic escape validated their ministry
Jesus healing on the Sabbath in John 5, Jesus heals a man on the Sabbath, the Pharisees considered this illegal, but Jesus knew the intent of the Sabbath
